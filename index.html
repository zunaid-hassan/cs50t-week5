What is climate change?

Climate change refers to the changing state of the Earth's atmosphere.

Historically, temperature of the planet fluctuated due to natural processes. Recently, however, there has been a drastic shift in Earth's atmosphere that is unrelated to natural causes. A prominent driver is global warming, with the Earth's average temperature rising 1.18°C since the 19th century, caused by the greenhouse effect.

What is the greenhouse effect?

The greenhouse effect is the process which traps heat in Earth's atmosphere, causing the temperature to rise. This is driven by increasing greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide produced by industrialization. This carbon dioxide rise correlates with rising temperatures, linking climate change to human activities. Global warming causes climate change, which has numerous adverse effects on the Earth, as formally defined by NASA:

"Climate change is a broad range of global phenomena created predominantly by burning fossil fuels, which add heat-trapping gases to Earth’s atmosphere. These phenomena include the increased temperature trends described by global warming, but also encompass changes such as sea-level rise; ice mass loss in Greenland, Antarctica, the Arctic and mountain glaciers worldwide; shifts in flower/plant blooming; and extreme weather events."
